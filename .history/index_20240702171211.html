The Great State of Ohio

With a population surpassing 11 million, Ohio stands as the seventh-most populous state in the United States. Officially incorporated into the Union on March 1, 1803, Ohio has a rich history and cultural diversity. The state capital, Columbus, is nestled in the central region of Ohio, surrounded by a vibrant metropolitan area.

Scarlet Carnation: The State Flower of Ohio

Experience the charm of the Buckeye State through the iconic Scarlet Carnation, the state flower. Decorating fields and gardens with vibrant red hues, the Scarlet Carnation holds a special place in the hearts of Ohioans. It symbolizes the memory of President William McKinley, who was born in Ohio.

    Scarlet Carnation most populated areas:

  1. Columbus
  2. Wooster
  3. Cleveland

Embraced as a symbol of natural beauty and a nod to the state's historical significance, the Scarlet Carnation is a floral emblem that transcends regional boundaries, inviting all to appreciate the enchanting and colorful essence of the Ohio landscape.
