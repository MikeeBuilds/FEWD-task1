Welcome to Columbus

Columbus OH, officially the capital city of Ohio, is the largest city in the state. Located in the heart of Ohio, Columbus ranks as the 14th-most populous city in the United States, with a 2020 population of 905,748.

Explore historical Cleveland, a city that marries its industrial past with a vibrant cultural scene. From the iconic West Side Market, which has served locals since 1912, to the historic Playhouse Square, the second-largest theater district in the U.S., Cleveland offers a rich tapestry of architectural and cultural landmarks. Wander through the stately homes of Millionaires' Row or visit the Cleveland Museum of Art, renowned for its extensive collection spanning 6,000 years. Nestled on the shores of Lake Erie, this city not only chronicles the rise of American industry but also celebrates a dynamic arts and music heritage, making it a fascinating destination for history enthusiasts.

Cleveland River Walk

Stroll along the Cleveland Riverwalk, where the Cuyahoga River’s storied waters flow through a revitalized urban landscape. This scenic path connects the Flats, a historic industrial area transformed into a vibrant entertainment district, with lush green spaces and picturesque views. Enjoy the eclectic mix of dining, from casual eateries to upscale restaurants, all offering stunning riverside seating. Discover public art installations and historical markers that narrate the river’s journey from infamous pollution to environmental renewal. As you walk, the juxtaposition of historic bridges and modern developments showcases Cleveland's commitment to blending heritage with innovation, making the Riverwalk a must-visit urban oasis.
